Chocolate Gulch Loop Trail

While living in Southern California, we never had occasion to enjoy Fall foliage, like we do here in Idaho.  We took advantage of the opportunity to drive to Sun Valley, ID to see some wonderful fall colors.  As this was our first trip to the home of the world-renowned Sun Valley Ski Resort, we were also impressed with the rustic feeling of ski towns Ketchum and Hailey.  These weren't just sleepy mountain communities either.  Along the stretch of Highway 75 leading up to the ski resort is a bustling population of over 12,000 people! 

The trail follows along Big Wood River and Fox Creek.  Even though we may have missed the peak of the fall colors, we were still treated to a dazzling display of yellows, golds, oranges, reds, and greens.  Cottonwoods and Aspens were main contibutors in this area.

Although not the most strenuous hike we've ever done, this hike was certainly the longest for the kids, clocking in at just under 5 miles.  They were able to do it in about 3 hours.  It won't be long before these guys are ready for some backpacking!
